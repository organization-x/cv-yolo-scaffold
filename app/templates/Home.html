Potholes are a never ending problem, they damage the rims, tires, and suspension of a car whenever they drive over them. Although they can always be filled, potholes will continue to form as they are the product of water weakening the asphalt and seeping into tiny cracks. The water within these cracks would eventually displace the asphalt and give birth to a brand new pothole.

Pothole Formation

Water from rain seeps into
roads through cracks

Under cold temperatures, 
the water freezes and expands

Ice melting would then leave moisture inside and form gaps, weakening the pavement

The pavement would then be no longer able to support vehicles and cracks under the pressure
